About Project Pulmonary

Project Pulmonary is a national environmental justice nonprofit dedicated to supporting firefighters exposed to chronic airborne carcinogens and respiratory toxins through community action and advocacy.

501(c)(3) Environmental Justice Firefighter Health Region-based Chapters

Mission

We mobilize communities to protect firefighter lung health through practical station support and advocacy.

What we do

We coordinate hydration & recovery drives, run the Letters for Lungs campaign, publish awareness content, and grow region-based chapters to sustain measurable local impact.

Why environmental justice

Wildfire smoke and toxic airborne exposure disproportionately impact workers and communities. Supporting firefighters is part of health equity and environmental justice.

Our approach

A simple model: local action, national awareness, measurable outcomes.

Local Chapters

Region-based chapters coordinate drives and partnerships in their communities.

Direct Station Support

Hydration and recovery resources delivered to fire stations with transparent reporting.

Advocacy

Letters for Lungs and awareness posts elevate visibility and urgency.

Collaboration

Work with aligned nonprofits and community partners to scale impact responsibly.
